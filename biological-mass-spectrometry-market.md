<p><p>Biological mass spectrometry encompasses various ionization techniques, notably Electrospray Ionization (ESI) and Matrix-Assisted Laser Desorption Ionization (MALDI). ESI is ideal for analyzing large biomolecules in solution, enabling soft ionization and facilitating high-throughput analyses. MALDI, on the other hand, excels in profiling proteins and peptides from solid samples, employing laser energy to create ions. Other market techniques include Chemical Ionization and Atmospheric Pressure Ionization, catering to diverse applications in proteomics, metabolomics, and drug development, thus expanding the market's scope.</p></p>
